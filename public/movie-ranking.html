Great Movies According to Me

My top 3 movie picks from Turkish cinema.

7. Koğuştaki Mucize

“7. Koğuştaki Mucize” tells the deeply emotional story of Memo, a mentally impaired father who lives with his young daughter Ova and his grandmother in a small Turkish town in the 1980s. Memo is wrongfully accused of murdering the daughter of a high-ranking military commander after a tragic accident. Despite his innocence and simple nature, he is convicted and imprisoned under harsh conditions. Inside prison, Memo’s gentle heart and love for his daughter gradually win over his cellmates. The film focuses on his fight for justice, the loyalty between diverse inmates, and his unbreakable bond with Ova as they both hope for a miracle that will reunite them. The movie is a Turkish adaptation of a South Korean film but emphasizes a more dramatic, emotional tone

Mucize

Mucize (which means “Miracle”) follows Mahir Yılmaz, a schoolteacher sent to a remote village in eastern Turkey in the 1960s. When he arrives, he discovers the village lacks a school and modern facilities. Despite resistance from locals, Mahir is determined to build a school and bring education to the children. With perseverance and ingenuity — including enlisting help from villagers who initially oppose him — he gradually earns their trust. The film highlights themes of education, cultural barriers, community solidarity, and personal transformation in a rural setting.

Aşk Tesadüfleri Sever

Aşk Tesadüfleri Sever (meaning Love Likes Coincidences) follows the lifelong, intertwined relationship of Özgür and Deniz, whose paths cross by chance from the moment they are born — when their parents collide on the way to the hospital — through childhood, adolescence, and adulthood. They keep meeting at pivotal moments in life, even as circumstances pull them apart. Set across decades and different cities in Türkiye, the film explores how fate, chance encounters, and unseen connections shape love and life. Nostalgic flashbacks and beautiful period details enrich the emotional arc of their journey.
